# ELI ESCOBAR & BOY CORDERO BRING HOUSE HEAT TO LE BAIN

**Le Bain, The Standard Hotel / March 18, 2026**

Wednesday night on the West Side. **Eli Escobar**—a name that should be familiar to anyone who's spent time in New York's house music scene—takes over Le Bain alongside **Boy Cordero** for a night of proper, disco-infused house music high above the Hudson River.

## THE OG

Eli Escobar is New York house music royalty. He's been holding down residencies, releasing tracks, and setting dancefloors on fire since the early 2000s. His sound is deeply rooted in disco, classic house, and the kind of party-starting energy that comes from decades behind the decks in this city.

What makes Eli special is his encyclopedic knowledge of dance music history combined with a selector's ear for what works right now. He can drop a disco edit from 1978 next to a house track from last week and make it sound like they were made for each other. His productions on labels like Night People NYC and Classic Music Company carry that same timeless quality.

## THE VENUE

Le Bain is an interesting choice for this bill. The rooftop space at The Standard Hotel has hosted countless memorable nights, offering views of the Hudson River and downtown Manhattan that few venues can match. While it's known for a more mixed crowd than your average underground spot, the sound system is serious and the room can get properly sweaty when the right DJ is playing.

There's something fitting about house music in this setting—disco's roots in glamorous nightlife, the connection between NYC's queer club history and spaces like this. Escobar understands that lineage better than most.

## BOY CORDERO

Boy Cordero has been making waves in NYC's house scene with a sound that bridges classic Chicago and New York influences with contemporary production. His sets are energetic, vocal-heavy, and designed for movement. Paired with Escobar, this should be a masterclass in house music selection.

## WHY YOU SHOULD GO

Midweek parties hit different. The crowd is there for the music, not just the scene. And when you get someone like Eli Escobar in a room with a view like Le Bain's, magic can happen. This is the kind of night where you might hear a record that sends you down a months-long digging journey, or a moment where the whole room connects over a perfect disco vocal.

House music was born in New York. Eli Escobar keeps that flame burning.
