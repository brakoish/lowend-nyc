# WIRE FESTIVAL 2026: KNOCKDOWN CENTER GOES ALL IN

**Knockdown Center, Queens / April 10-12, 2026 / Three Days**

The announcement hit my inbox at 9 AM on a Tuesday. WIRE Festival 2026. Three days. Knockdown Center. And a lineup that reads like a who's-who of the underground's current moment.

This isn't just another festival. WIRE has become the definitive NYC electronic music gathering — the one weekend where the global scene converges on a warehouse in Maspeth and things get properly unhinged.

## THE LINEUP

**Friday** leans heavy into peak-time techno. We're talking **Dax J**, **999999999**, and **I Hate Models** — the kind of artists who play at 140 BPM and don't apologize for it. The Basement stage will be a sweatbox. Plan accordingly.

**Saturday** splits the difference. **Peggy Gou** headlines the main room (expect a three-hour set, she's been stretching them out lately), while **Overmono** and **Fred again..** bring their live A/V setups to the second stage. This is the crossover night — the one your friends who don't usually go to raves will want to hit.

**Sunday** is where it gets interesting. **Oneohtrix Point Never** is doing a live score to some Tarkovsky footage. **Holly Herndon** is presenting her AI choir piece. **Skee Mask** is closing with a five-hour ambient set. It's the experimental day, the palate cleanser, the reason WIRE isn't just another techno festival.

## THE VENUE

Knockdown Center has spent the last two years upgrading their infrastructure. New Funktion-One rig in the main room. Improved HVAC (thank god). Better bar setups so you're not missing half a set waiting for a drink.

The multi-room format is what makes this work. Five stages running simultaneously means you can curate your own experience. Want four hours of hard techno? Stay in Basement. Need a breather? The outdoor courtyard has a ambient stage and actual seating.

## PRACTICAL NOTES

**Getting there**: The shuttle from Williamsburg is back — $15 round trip, runs every 30 minutes. Otherwise it's the L to Jefferson and a 15-minute walk. Don't try to Uber home at 4 AM unless you want to pay surge pricing.

**Timing**: Doors at noon, but the serious programming doesn't start until 4 PM. If you're trying to catch a specific artist, check the set times the day before — WIRE releases them via Instagram Stories.

**What to bring**: Earplugs (the new system is LOUD), portable charger, layers for the outdoor areas. The warehouse gets hot even in April.

## THE VERDICT

WIRE is the one festival on the NYC calendar that justifies the hype. It's expensive, it's exhausting, and by Sunday night you'll question every life decision that led you to a warehouse in Queens at 3 AM. But it's also the closest thing we have to a proper European festival experience — three days of dedicated programming across multiple stages, with curation that actually makes sense.

If you can only do one day: **Saturday** for the energy, **Sunday** for the weirdness. But the full three-day pass is worth it if your liver can handle it.

**Rating: 8.5/10** (pending actual attendance, but the lineup earns the score)
