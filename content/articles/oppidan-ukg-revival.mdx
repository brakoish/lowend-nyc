# Oppidan brings the UK garage revival nobody expected

Origin: South London | Current: Global | Years Active: 2010s-Present

A South London producer is filling rooms in Bushwick. The UK Garage revival has found its American foothold, and Oppidan is holding the door.

## From schoolyard CDs to international bills

She started at nine years old. Burning CDs. Selling them at school. By twelve she was producing on Logic, the same software she still uses today. The DIY ethos of UK Garage wasn't something she read about, it was how she grew up.

That upbringing shows in the music. Oppidan blends classic 2-step with 4x4 NUKG and bass influences. It's familiar enough for the purists and fresh enough for the newcomers. Her bootleg rework of Adam F's DnB classic "Circles" caught attention from Disclosure and Conducta. The co-signs matter in this scene.

## The viral moment

Sammy Virji's "Shapes" remix changed her trajectory. It went viral. Suddenly she was on the radar of the biggest name in UKG right now. That led to management, she now shares a team with Virji himself.

Triple J called her "one of the hottest artists coming out of the United Kingdom." Insomniac labeled her "one of the fastest rising names in UK Garage." The recognition is coming from both sides of the Atlantic.

## Brooklyn proved it

A Reddit post from March 2025 tells the story: "I saw her in Brooklyn a few weeks ago and she absolutely blew me away."

That's the test. UK Garage is a London sound. It was born in the UK, evolved in the UK, and stayed largely UK-centric for decades. Getting it to work in Brooklyn, in Bushwick specifically, means translating culture, not just BPMs.

The Boiler Room set at SYSTEM: Bristol showed her range. The Brooklyn show proved her export potential. Oppidan isn't just playing UKG for UK audiences. She's showing Americans why this sound matters.

## The releases that built it

Night Bass. 24 Garage Girls. Kiwi Rekords. These are the labels that matter in UKG right now, and Oppidan has placed music on all of them.

"hold tight" with camoufly dropped in 2025. "FRESH AND NEW" with Frisco, BBK member, grime royalty, came in July 2024. She's not just collecting features. She's building a catalog that spans the UK bass music spectrum.

## Why her, why now

Female producers are still underrepresented in UKG. That's a fact, not an angle. Oppidan's rise matters because it challenges the default image of who makes this music.

But it's not charity. The Reddit comment says it best: "Oppidan is an amazing DJ, she's so good at blending different beats together without making it feel forced." The skill is there. The taste is there. The work ethic, burning CDs at nine, still grinding, is there.

## The revival is real

UK Garage never died. But it's having a moment. Eliza Rose. Sammy Virji. Interplanetary Criminal. The sound is back in the mainstream conversation, and a new generation is discovering why 2-step mattered in the first place.

Oppidan represents the next wave. Artists who grew up with the sound, who studied the masters, who are now evolving it for 2025 and beyond. Her blend of classic and modern, of 2-step and NUKG and bass, is where the genre is heading.

The UK Garage revival nobody expected? Maybe. But Oppidan expected it. She's been preparing for this since she was nine years old.
