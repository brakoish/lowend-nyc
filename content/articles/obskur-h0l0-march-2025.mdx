# Obskür at H0L0: The Irish Invasion Queens Actually Needed

I've been waiting for this one.

When Obskür announced their March 17th stop at H0L0, I cleared my calendar. The Irish duo, Lorcan McCarthy and Faustas Astrauskas, have been running hot since 2019, and their first proper Ridgewood warehouse set feels like a rite of passage. If you've been sleeping on them, wake up.

## What They Actually Sound Like

Obskür don't do polite. Their tracks hit like a pint glass hitting pub floor: sharp, sudden, hard to ignore. We're talking 909s that punch through concrete, hip-hop samples pulled from late-night pirate radio, and a rawness most house acts scrubbed out years ago.

"Bayside" still gets play in my sets, but the *Pure Evil* EP on Shall Not Fade is what locked them in. "Nando" and the title track take UK garage bones and rough them up with enough attitude that Pete Tong and Eats Everything started paying attention. Annie Mac too. Gorgon City as well.

660,000 monthly Spotify listeners. 10,000 daily SoundCloud plays. These guys aren't a secret anymore. But hearing them in an actual warehouse, this specific warehouse, hits different than any festival slot.

## The Room: H0L0 Knows

H0L0's been at 1090 Wyckoff since July 2017. Eight years of holding down Queens nightlife without turning into a bottle service nightmare. 800 capacity. No security theater. Full bar. They actually mean it when they say the space is for "outsider communities."

Ridgewood sits right on the Bushwick border. L to Halsey, M to Myrtle-Wyckoff. I've caught everything in that room from noise experiments to proper techno workouts. Sound system punches above its weight. For a duo who cut their teeth in Dublin basements and want intimate US rooms, it's a match.

## Openers Worth Showing Up For

Armii1n and Ford Scott round out the bill. Ford Scott's been building in NYC with a sound that lives between house and breaks. Check his SoundCloud if you don't believe me. 2.4K monthly listeners. Support from RICCI and Mac Briggs. The guy knows how to warm a room without playing it safe.

Armii1n I'm less familiar with, and that's fine. H0L0's curation has earned my trust. They don't book filler. Given the venue's focus on queer artists and people who take sonic risks, I'm expecting something that breaks the warm-up mold.

## Why This Show Actually Matters

Obskür could be playing bigger rooms. They've got the numbers, the cosigns, the festival slots. But they picked H0L0 for a St. Patrick's Day weekender. That says something about priorities. They want proximity. They want sweat. They want a room where the kick drum moves air instead of getting swallowed by some cavernous field.

I've watched too many acts blow up and lose the intimacy that made them interesting. Obskür haven't made that mistake yet. Their tracks still sound like 3 AM basement sessions, not main stage EDC fodder. This H0L0 show is a statement. They're still here for the real thing.

## Bottom Line

If you're in NYC and you care where house music is going, be at this show. Period. Obskür's rising profile plus H0L0's actual community focus plus a Tuesday night slot that filters out tourists makes this one of the month's best bookings.

This will sell out. Get there early for Ford Scott. Post up center-floor before Obskür drop "Bayside" and the room loses its collective mind.

**9/10** — Essential for house heads. Mandatory if you're tracking the UK-to-NYC pipeline.
