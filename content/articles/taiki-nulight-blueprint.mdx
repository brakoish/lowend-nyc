# Taiki Nulight is still evolving

Origin: UK | Current: Global | Years Active: 2008-Present

Taiki Nulight doesn't sit still. While bass house became a formula, he was already moving on. That's been the pattern for twenty years. Get good at something, then find the next edge.

## The origin story

Erka Chinbayer started producing at 14. Not in some scene hub, just a bedroom in the UK, experimenting with the deeper corners of 140 BPM wobble. This was around 2008, when dubstep was still underground and My Nu Leng, Cause & Affect, and Woz were pushing similar territory.

He wasn't solo at first. The original Nulight was a duo. When they split, Chinbayer kept the name and added Taiki. The solo project started around 2014-2015.

## The breakthrough

The real shift happened when he connected with AC Slater and the Night Bass crew. US bass house was bubbling, and Taiki's UK garage roots gave him a different angle. He started touring the States regularly, building a following that crossed the Atlantic divide.

His sound stood out because it wasn't pure US bass house. It carried UK DNA (grime, dubstep, garage) but packaged for bigger rooms. That hybrid approach became his signature.

## The sound

What makes Taiki Nulight distinct:

Genre fluidity: He moves between bass house, dubstep, and garage without sounding like he's trying too hard. The transitions feel natural because he actually knows all three worlds.

UK bass weight: Even his house tracks hit with that low-end pressure UK producers grow up on. It's physical music.

Vocal chops and MC energy: Tracks like Kush (VIP) with Dread MC show his comfort working with grime-style vocals.

It's bass music for people who get bored staying in one lane.

## The evolution

Not everyone loved every move. When he dipped back into dubstep with the Stay in Your Lane EP in 2024, some bass house purists probably scratched their heads. But that's the point. He's never been interested in staying in one genre's good graces.

The Mixmag feature from 2024 called him a bass shapeshifter. That's accurate. He's earned the right to move around because he's put in work across multiple scenes.

## The now

Current status: Active and touring. His Cheeky Bugger Tour hit Bowery Ballroom in NYC in 2026, a proper room for his sound. He's also done The Caverns, Rinse FM sessions, and keeps dropping remixes (Skrillex, Becky Hill, NLE Choppa, Hamdi).

Recent moves:
- Stay in Your Lane EP (2024): Return to dubstep/grime territory
- Remixes for major artists across genres
- Sample pack with Splice/Loopmasters teaching his bass house approach
- Consistent touring schedule in US and UK

## The essentials

3 tracks to start with:
1. Kush (VIP) ft. Dread MC (Night Bass): The perfect entry point. UK MC energy over US bass house production.
2. Stay in Your Lane (2024): Shows his dubstep roots are still alive and current.
3. Lose My Mind: Classic bass house with his signature groove.

Where to see him:
- Check Resident Advisor or his Instagram for upcoming dates
- Recent NYC appearance: Bowery Ballroom (2026)

## The take

Taiki Nulight matters because he bridges scenes without diluting either. He's not a US producer playing at UK sounds, or a UK producer chasing US trends. He actually understands both, and that authenticity comes through in the music.

Two decades in, he's still evolving. The Stay in Your Lane EP wasn't nostalgia. It was a statement that he can still operate in the 140 space at a high level. Meanwhile, his bass house catalog keeps growing, and his remix work shows he can adapt to whatever's current.

Rating: 8/10 (for consistent quality and genuine evolution)

In a scene full of copycats and trend-chasers, Taiki Nulight has managed to stay original without being obscure. That's harder than it looks.
