# Obskür Brings Dublin Heat to H0L0's Ridgewood Warehouse

I've been waiting for this one.

When Obskür announced their March 17th stop at H0L0, I immediately cleared my calendar. The Irish duo—Lorcan McCarthy and Faustas Astrauskas—have been on a tear since they emerged from Dublin in 2019, and their first proper Ridgewood warehouse set feels like a rite of passage. If you've been sleeping on them, this is your wake-up call.

## The Sound: Raw, Rowdy, and Unapologetically Irish

Obskür don't do polite. Their productions hit like a pint glass on a pub floor—sharp, sudden, and impossible to ignore. I'm talking hard-hitting 909s that punch through the mix, slick hip-hop samples that feel lifted from late-night pirate radio, and a rawness that most polished house acts have scrubbed clean.

Their breakout "Bayside" still gets rotation in my sets, but it's the *Pure Evil* EP on Shall Not Fade that really cemented their status. Tracks like the title cut and "Nando" showcase what they do best: take classic UK garage and house frameworks, then rough them up with enough attitude to make Pete Tong and Eats Everything take notice. Which they did—both have been vocal supporters, along with Annie Mac and Gorgon City.

With 660,000 monthly Spotify listeners and 10,000 daily SoundCloud streams, these guys aren't some underground secret anymore. But there's something about hearing them in a proper warehouse context—specifically *this* warehouse—that hits different.

## The Venue: H0L0 Gets It

H0L0 has been holding it down at 1090 Wyckoff Avenue since July 2017, and in that time they've become essential to Queens' nightlife ecosystem. 800-capacity, no security theater, full bar, and a commitment to outsider communities that actually means something. This isn't a bottle service playground—it's a space devoted to "sonic exploration and expression," which is marketing speak for "you can actually take risks here."

The Ridgewood location puts it right on the border of Bushwick, accessible via the L to Halsey or the M to Myrtle-Wyckoff. I've seen everything from experimental noise to proper techno workouts in that room, and the sound system punches above its weight. For a duo like Obskür—who cut their teeth in Dublin clubs and have been itching for more intimate US rooms—this is a perfect match.

## The Support: Don't Sleep on the Openers

Armii1n and Ford Scott round out the bill, and both deserve your attention. Ford Scott's been building momentum in NYC with a sound that sits comfortably between house and breaks—check his SoundCloud if you need convincing. The guy's got 2.4K monthly listeners and support from names like RICCI and Mac Briggs, but more importantly, he understands how to warm up a room without playing it safe.

Armii1n I'm less familiar with personally, but that's part of the appeal. H0L0's curation has earned my trust over the years—they don't book filler. Given the venue's focus on queer artists and sonic risk-takers, I'm expecting something that challenges the standard warm-up playbook.

## Why This Matters

Here's the thing: Obskür could easily be playing bigger rooms. They've got the streaming numbers, the industry cosigns, the festival slots. But choosing H0L0 for a St. Patrick's Day weekender says something about their priorities. They want proximity. They want sweat. They want a room where the kick drum actually moves air instead of getting lost in a cavernous festival field.

I've seen too many acts blow up and immediately lose the intimacy that made them interesting. Obskür haven't made that mistake yet. Their productions still feel like they were made for 3 AM basement sessions, not main stage EDC slots. This H0L0 show is a statement: we're still here for the real thing.

## The Verdict

If you're in NYC and you care about where house music is headed, you should be at this show. Full stop. The combination of Obskür's rising profile, H0L0's community-focused ethos, and a Tuesday night slot that guarantees a committed crowd (not tourists) makes this one of the most compelling bookings of the month.

My prediction? This sells out. My recommendation? Get there early, catch Ford Scott's set, and position yourself center-floor before Obskür drop "Bayside" and the room collectively loses its mind.

**Rating: 9/10** — Essential for house heads, mandatory for anyone tracking the UK-to-NYC pipeline.
