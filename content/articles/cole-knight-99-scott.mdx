# COLE KNIGHT PROVED SHE'S MORE THAN AN OPENER AT 99 SCOTT

**99 Scott, Brooklyn / March 13, 2026 / Gray Area**

Opening for Pegassi at 99 Scott, **Cole Knight** could have played it safe. She didn't.

## THE SET

The Miami-based DJ, producer, and vocalist has been on a serious run. Releases on Life and Death, Higher Ground, and Factory 93 Records. Sets at Coachella, Exit Festival, Day Zero Tulum, DC-10 and UNVRS in Ibiza, Sisyphos in Berlin. She arrived in Brooklyn with nothing to prove and played like it.

Her sound moved with intention: acid lines over 4/4 kicks, hard groove that kept pushing without ever getting monotonous. There were flashes of UKG and breaks in there too. The set built steadily from the 10 PM open until Pegassi took over, and by the time she stepped off, the room was already at a level that most headliners would be happy to inherit.

With her upcoming single **"Body Control"** on Nervous Records and the **Knight Club** concept she launched at Factory Town during Miami Art Basel, Cole Knight is building something that goes beyond just DJ sets.

## STANDOUT MOMENTS

- An acid-laced stretch around 11 PM that locked in the early arrivals
- Seamless genre-blending that never felt forced
- The transition into Pegassi's set: handled like a pro, no ego

## THE VERDICT

Opening slots are often thankless. Cole Knight made this one count. If you're sleeping on her, the window is closing. She's got the credits, the releases, and now the live proof.

**Rating: 8/10**
