# LOWEND NYC — Engagement-Focused Content Templates

These templates are designed to maximize reader engagement, shares, and community building.

## Template Overview

| Template | Best For | Engagement Type |
|----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Artist Profile** | Rising stars, controversial figures, scene veterans | Comments, shares, debate |
| **Scene Analysis** | Trends, industry shifts, cultural moments | Discussion, shares, authority building |
| **List Format** | Roundups, predictions, "best of" | Shares, saves, tags |
| **Venue Spotlight** | New spaces, iconic rooms, underappreciated spots | Local engagement, nostalgia |
| **Event Preview** | Can't-miss shows, unique lineups | Ticket clicks, attendance, anticipation |
| **Hot Take/Debate** | Divisive topics, unpopular opinions | Comments, debate, viral potential |

---

## Engagement Strategies Built In

### 1. **Strong Openings**
Every template starts with a hook that:
- Makes a bold claim
- Asks a provocative question
- Establishes stakes immediately

### 2. **Clear Structure**
- Scannable sections with bold headers
- Bullet points for easy reading
- "The Verdict" section for your opinion

### 3. **Engagement Prompts**
Every template ends with:
- A direct question to readers
- Invitation to comment/share
- Promise of community features (roundups, features)

### 4. **Shareable Elements**
- Pull quotes for social media
- Rating systems (X/10)
- Lists that are easy to screenshot
- Strong opinions that spark debate

### Weekly Rotation (Example)

| Day | Format | Purpose |
|-----|--------|---------|
| Monday | Event Preview | Drive ticket sales, build anticipation |
| Wednesday | Artist Profile | Deep engagement, evergreen content |
| Friday | List or Hot Take | Weekend discussion, viral potential |
| Sunday | Scene Analysis | Thought leadership, newsletter feature |

### Monthly Rhythm

- **Week 1**: Event previews for upcoming shows
- **Week 2**: Artist profile + venue spotlight
- **Week 3**: Scene analysis or hot take
- **Week 4**: List format (roundup, predictions)

---

## Writing Tips for Engagement

### Do:
- Take strong positions (readers respond to opinions)
- Use specific examples and data
- Include quotes from artists/promoters
- Ask direct questions throughout
- Reference current events/trends
- Use "you" and "we" to build community

### Don't:
- Be neutral (boring = no engagement)
- Write generic descriptions
- Ignore counter-arguments
- Forget the engagement prompt at the end
- Use passive voice
- Hedge your opinions

## Headline Formulas That Work

### Artist Profiles
- "[Artist] Is [Doing Something Unexpected]"
- "How [Artist] Went From [Humble] to [Success]"
- "The [Genre] Producer Everyone's Sleeping On"

### Scene Analysis
- "[Trend]: [Provocative Question]?"
- "What [Event/Change] Means for [Audience]"
- "The [Number] [Things] [Changing/Defining] [Scene]"

### Lists
- "[Number] [Things] [Verb] [Scene Element] Right Now"
- "The [Adjective] [Noun] in [Location], Ranked"
- "[Number] Predictions for [Scene] in [Year]"

### Hot Takes
- "[Controversial Statement]. Here's Why."
- "Can We Talk About [Topic]?"
- "[Popular Opinion] Is Wrong. Here's the Truth."

Track these metrics for each piece:

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------------|

| **Time on Page** | Content quality, engagement depth |
| **Scroll Depth** | Whether people finish the piece |

## Quick Start

1. **Choose your format** based on what's happening in the scene
2. **Fill in the brackets** — the structure guides your research
3. **Take a position** — don't be neutral
4. **Add the engagement prompt** — invite comments/shares
5. **Publish and monitor** — respond to early comments to boost engagement
