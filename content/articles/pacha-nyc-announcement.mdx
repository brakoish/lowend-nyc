# PACHA NEW YORK IS COMING: WHAT THE BROOKLYN MIRAGE REBRAND MEANS FOR NYC NIGHTLIFE

**Editorial / March 10, 2026**

The Brooklyn Mirage is dead. **Pacha New York** is coming in its place. Here's what we know and what it means.

## THE STORY

Dubai-based conglomerate **FIVE Holdings**, which owns the global Pacha franchise, has finalized acquisition of the former Brooklyn Mirage site and announced plans to launch **Pacha New York in June 2026**. A new $500,000 construction proposal was recently filed with the NYC Department of Buildings.

The move comes after **Avant Gardner's troubled final years**: merchant cash advance debt, the Electric Zoo disaster of 2023, and a gradual erosion of the community trust that made the venue special in the first place. The outdoor paradise that once hosted the biggest names in electronic music became a cautionary tale about corporate nightlife.

## WHAT IT MEANS

Pacha is one of the most iconic nightlife brands in the world. The original Ibiza location set the template for superclubs. A NYC outpost with the Brooklyn Mirage's infrastructure (that sound system, those sightlines) could be exactly what the city needs.

But the skeptics have points too. The Mirage thrived because it felt like Brooklyn's venue. A global franchise coming in could sanitize the exact thing that made it work. Will Pacha NYC book the underground artists that built the Mirage's reputation, or will it lean purely into mainstream headliners?

## OUR TAKE

We'll reserve judgment until June. The bones of that venue are incredible. If Pacha respects the underground while bringing their operational expertise and booking budget, NYC wins. If they turn it into another bottle-service megaclub, the scene will find somewhere else. It always does.

**We'll be there opening weekend. Stay tuned.**
