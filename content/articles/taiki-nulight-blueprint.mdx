# TAIKI NULIGHT'S BASS HOUSE BLUEPRINT

**Origin: UK | Based: Global | Active Since: Early 2010s**

Erka Chinbayer. Taiki Nulight to the rest of us. didn't arrive at bass house through the obvious route. While most producers in the genre came up through EDM or festival culture, he cut his teeth in the UK's golden era of dubstep and garage. That background is what makes his production hit different. There's weight underneath the bounce.

## THE ORIGIN

The UK underground in the early 2010s was a specific time and place. Dubstep was fragmenting. some of it going stadium, some of it going deeper. Garage was having a quiet renaissance. Grime was everywhere. Taiki came up in that soup, absorbing all of it.

"I was DJing and producing for many years in very different circles," he's said in interviews. That versatility isn't an accident. it's the whole point. His Splice sample pack, released through Night Bass, is literally called "Origins" because the sounds draw from every corner of UK bass music.

Pete Tong, Annie Mac, and Mistajam at BBC Radio 1 all championed him early. That's not the festival DJ pipeline. that's the UK tastemaker circuit. Different credibility entirely.

## THE SOUND

What Taiki does that most bass house producers don't: he treats the low end like a dubstep producer and the groove like a garage head. The result is tracks that hit harder than typical house but move better than typical bass music.

His recent work has been shifting. The Mixmag feature ("The Mix 038") caught him in a moment where he was openly returning to 140 BPM territory. dubstep, halftime, the heavier end of the spectrum. The mix he delivered was, in their words, "head-rattling basslines and silky-smooth dubstep in a homage to his time in the 140 scene."

That evolution matters. A lot of producers find their lane and stay there. Taiki keeps moving. bass house, dubstep, garage, trap. His 1001Tracklists profile shows recent sets filed under "Dubstep, Trap" alongside his bass house catalog. He's not locked in.

## THE NIGHT BASS CONNECTION

Night Bass is the label and party series that became synonymous with bass house in America. Taiki has been a core part of that ecosystem. releasing music, playing the nights, contributing to the label's identity.

But Night Bass isn't his whole story. He's bigger than one label. His production credits and remixes span multiple imprints, and his touring schedule in 2025 included everything from The Caverns to Rinse FM sessions. That range. underground radio to festival stages. is rare.

## THE NYC MOMENT

His Bowery Ballroom show on the "Cheeky Bugger Tour" was a statement. Bowery isn't a typical electronic music room. it's a rock venue with history. Bringing bass house there was a deliberate choice, and it worked. The room was full, the sound system handled it, and the crowd was a mix of heads who'd followed him from the dubstep days and newer fans who found him through Night Bass.

## THE BLUEPRINT

Here's what Taiki Nulight actually built: a production style that's flexible enough to move between genres without losing identity. You always know it's him. the bass weight, the UK swing, the attention to groove over spectacle. But you never know exactly what he's going to play.

That's the blueprint. Not "bass house producer" as a fixed identity, but bass music fluency as a foundation for going wherever the sound takes you. In a scene that rewards specialization, he's proof that range still works.
