# CARL COX RETURNS TO BROOKLYN WITH NICOLE MOUDABER & ILARIO ALICANTE

**Brooklyn Storehouse, Brooklyn Navy Yard / March 21, 2026**

Saturday night at the Navy Yard. **Teksupport** brings three of techno's most formidable names to Brooklyn Storehouse for a showcase that reads like a festival headliner list. **Carl Cox**. **Nicole Moudaber**. **Ilario Alicante**. This isn't just a party—it's a statement of intent.

## THE KING

Carl Cox needs no introduction, but I'll give him one anyway. The man has been at the forefront of electronic music for over three decades. From his legendary residency at Space Ibiza to his current "Awesome Soundwave" imprint, Cox has remained relevant by never standing still. His sets are masterclasses in energy management—knowing exactly when to push, when to pull back, and when to detonate the dancefloor.

What makes Cox special in 2026 is that he's not resting on legacy. His recent productions have embraced the harder, faster techno sound that's dominating warehouses worldwide, while maintaining the funk and soul that made him famous. Seeing him in a Brooklyn warehouse rather than a festival mainstage is a rare treat—you get the intimate, extended set experience that true heads crave.

## THE FIRST LADY OF MOOD

Nicole Moudaber has earned every bit of her reputation as one of techno's most consistent heavy hitters. Her "Mood" brand has become synonymous with dark, driving, emotionally charged techno that works equally well at 2 AM or 6 AM. Her residency at DC-10 and her global touring schedule have made her a fixture in the world's best booths.

Moudaber's sound sits perfectly between Cox's classicism and Alicante's modernism. She understands tension and release better than almost anyone in the game. When she takes the decks at Brooklyn Storehouse, expect a journey through the shadows—industrial textures, hypnotic grooves, and moments of genuine transcendence.

## THE RISING FORCE

Ilario Alicante represents the new generation of techno artists who grew up on the legends sharing this bill. The Italian producer has been on a tear, releasing on Drumcode, Cocoon, and his own Virgo label. His sound is sleek, powerful, and designed for big rooms—but it never loses the underground edge that got him noticed.

Alicante's rise has been meteoric but deserved. He's paid his dues in Berlin clubs and Ibiza terraces, developing a sound that's both contemporary and timeless. Sharing a bill with Cox and Moudaber is a passing of the torch moment, and you can bet he'll be bringing his absolute best.

## THE VENUE

Brooklyn Storehouse has quickly established itself as one of NYC's premier warehouse venues. The industrial setting, the Funktion-One sound system, and the dedicated crowd all contribute to an atmosphere that feels distinctly underground despite the scale. For a lineup of this magnitude, there's no better room in the city.

## WHY YOU SHOULD GO

This is the kind of lineup that defines a season. Three generations of techno excellence, one room, one night. Whether you're a longtime Cox devotee, a Moudaber convert, or curious about Alicante's buzz, this is unmissable. Teksupport continues to prove why they're the city's most important promoters for underground electronic music.

The warehouse is calling. Answer it.
