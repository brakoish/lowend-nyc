# TAIKI NULIGHT'S CHEEKY BUGGER TOUR SHAKES BOWERY BALLROOM

**Bowery Ballroom, Manhattan / March 14, 2026**

Friday night on the Lower East Side. **Taiki Nulight** brought his **Cheeky Bugger Tour** to the Bowery Ballroom with **Rohaan** opening. The venue's balcony was sardine-packed by the time Taiki stepped up.

## THE SET

If you know Taiki Nulight's production, you know it's a melting pot of UK dancefloor DNA: bass, garage, house, and everything in between, stamped with a sound that's instantly recognizable as his. Live, that identity is even more pronounced.

He opened with deep, rolling garage that gradually pushed into heavier bass house territory. The track selection moved with purpose, weaving through 2-step patterns, 4x4 kicks, and those signature Taiki bass stabs that hit you in the chest. The Bowery Ballroom's sound system, usually calibrated for indie rock, handled the sub frequencies surprisingly well.

Rohaan's opening set was a revelation for anyone who hadn't caught him live. His production credits speak for themselves, and his DJ set showed the same attention to energy management, building from minimal bass house into full-throttle territory.

## STANDOUT MOMENTS

- Rohaan's opening 45 minutes, a masterclass in building tension
- Taiki dropping a garage flip of a classic R&B vocal that sent the balcony crowd over the edge
- The bass house to breaks switch-up around midnight that caught everyone off guard
- Closing stretch: pure UK energy, the kind of set that makes Americans understand why garage never died

## THE VERDICT

Taiki Nulight is a pioneer for a reason. His sound defined bass house before the genre had a Beatport category, and his live sets prove he's still pushing it forward. The Cheeky Bugger Tour is appropriately named: cheeky, unexpected, and impossible not to move to. Bowery Ballroom was the right room for this.

**Rating: 8.5/10**
