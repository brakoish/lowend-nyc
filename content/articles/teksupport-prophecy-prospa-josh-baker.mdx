# TEKSUPPORT X PROPHECY: PROSPA & JOSH BAKER TAKE OVER BROOKLYN STOREHOUSE

**Brooklyn Storehouse, Brooklyn Navy Yard / March 20, 2026**

Friday night at the Navy Yard. **Teksupport** and **Prophecy** join forces for a showcase that bridges the gap between progressive house's emotional peaks and techno's driving momentum. Headlining: UK duo **Prospa** alongside rising star **Josh Baker**.

## THE COLLABORATION

This event represents something important in NYC's electronic music landscape—the continued partnership between Teksupport (the city's premier warehouse rave promoters) and Prophecy (the brand behind some of the most forward-thinking progressive and melodic events). Together, they're bringing a distinctly UK-flavored lineup to Brooklyn.

## THE ARTISTS

**Prospa** have been on a tear. The Leeds duo burst onto the scene with "Prayer," a track that somehow managed to be both a festival anthem and a credible underground cut. Since then, they've refined their sound—less big-room, more nuanced, but never losing that emotional core that made people pay attention in the first place.

Their DJ sets are journeys through progressive house, melodic techno, and the spaces in between. Expect moments of genuine beauty mixed with proper dancefloor weapons. They're the kind of act that can make a warehouse feel like a cathedral.

**Josh Baker** represents the new generation of UK selectors making waves internationally. His sound sits comfortably between house and techno, with a keen ear for vocals and melodies that stick. He's been a regular at Prophecy events and brings the kind of energy that builds crowds, not just plays to them.

## THE VENUE

Brooklyn Storehouse has become the go-to spot for events of this caliber. The industrial setting, the serious sound system, and the dedicated crowd all contribute to an atmosphere that feels distinctly New York while drawing from global influences.

## WHY YOU SHOULD GO

If you're into the intersection of progressive house and melodic techno—think labels like Anjunadeep, Rose Avenue, and the deeper side of Drumcode—this is your night. Prospa are at an interesting point in their career, moving beyond their breakout moment into more refined territory. Seeing them in a warehouse setting rather than a festival stage should reveal new dimensions to their sound.
