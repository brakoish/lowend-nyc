# Taiki Nulight's Bass House Blueprint

**Origin:** United Kingdom | **Active Since:** 2012 | **Sound:** UK garage DNA, US bass house muscle

I first caught Taiki Nulight at a Night Bass showcase in Brooklyn around 2016. Output was still open then—remember Output?—and the room was packed with kids who'd come for AC Slater but stayed for this UK producer they'd never heard of. By the end of his set, the crowd was converted. That cross-Atlantic alchemy—UK garage swing meeting US bass weight—has been his signature ever since.

## The Foundation: UK Roots

Taiki Nulight is the performance alias of Erka Chinbayar, a UK producer who came up in the early 2010s bass music scramble. His first releases dropped in 2012 as "Taiki & Nulight"—the *Coming Up EP* on Dutch label Subway and the *Late Nights* EP on Cheap Thrills Records. These early records showed a producer already comfortable with low-end pressure, pulling from dubstep and UK garage without pledging allegiance to either.

The split from his original partner came around 2014-2015. Chinbayar kept the name, shortening it to Taiki Nulight. The solo era started with a sharper focus: take UK bass aesthetics and make them work on bigger stages.

## The Night Bass Connection

The turning point was his 2015 link-up with AC Slater's Night Bass imprint. The *LTNS* EP with Low Steppa (Night Bass, 2015) announced him as a serious player in the US bass house movement. But unlike a lot of UK producers who struggled to translate their sound Stateside, Taiki's music fit naturally. The Night Bass roster—AC Slater, Jack Beats, Wax Motif—became his creative home.

Key Night Bass releases:
- **"LTNS"** (with Low Steppa, 2015) - The breakthrough
- **"My Peoples"** (with AC Slater, 2016) - Cementing the partnership
- **"Murky Manor"** (2018) - His debut album, 11 tracks of pure bass architecture
- **"Soundwave"** EP (2019) - Peak-era Night Bass energy

The Night Bass connection wasn't just about releases—it opened up US touring. Taiki became a regular on the American festival circuit, playing EDC, Nocturnal Wonderland, and club shows across the country. That transatlantic presence is rare; most UK bass producers never crack the US market this thoroughly.

## The Sound: What Makes It Work

Taiki Nulight's production sits at a specific intersection:

**UK Garage Swing:** Those shuffled hi-hats and off-beat bass stabs come straight from the 2-step and UKG playbook. He grew up on this stuff, and you can hear it in the groove.

**US Bass Weight:** The subs hit harder than traditional UK garage. He's producing for big rooms, Funktion-One stacks, crowds that want physical impact.

**Grime Vocals:** Collaborations with MCs like Dread MC ("Kush VIP") and Scrufizzer ("Skank N Flex" with Wax Motif) keep the UK street-level energy intact.

**Genre Fluidity:** He'll drop a dubstep tune in a bass house set and make it feel natural. The 2024 *Stay In Your Lane* EP on Polydor saw him return to 140 BPM territory without stumbling.

## Evolution & Recent Moves (2024-2025)

The past two years have been busy. After a string of singles on Cr2, Divided Souls, and Revealed Recordings, Taiki landed a major label deal with Polydor/Universal Music UK—a significant step up from the indie dance world.

**2024 Highlights:**
- **"The Box"** (Deadbeats, June 2024) - Zeds Dead's label, showing his reach into the US bass music elite
- **"Stay In Your Lane"** EP (Polydor, December 2024) - Five tracks that balance his bass house present with dubstep roots
- **"Stay in Your Lane"** (with P Money & Jolie P, September 2024) - The title track with UK grime legend P Money

**2025 Momentum:**
- **"Out Of Control"** (with borne, February 2025, Polydor) - A massive collab that shows his sound scaling up for mainstream audiences
- **"Innocence 2025"** (with NERO, March 2025, Chaos/Universal) - This is huge. Remixing/reworking NERO's classic for a new generation
- **"142 GODZILLA"** (April 2025) - Solo material keeping the pressure on

The NERO collaboration says a lot. NERO were dubstep royalty in the early 2010s; for Taiki to get tapped for an official rework of "Innocence" shows the respect he's earned across generations of UK bass producers.

## Key Collaborations

Taiki's network runs deep:
- **AC Slater** - The Night Bass connection that changed everything
- **Jack Beats** - "King of the Monsters" (2022) on Orient Records
- **Wax Motif** - "Skank N Flex" (2020) with Scrufizzer, a Divided Souls highlight
- **Stanton Warriors** - "What You Got Now" (2019) bridging breaks and bass house
- **Low Steppa** - The 2015 link-up that started the Night Bass era
- **NERO** - The 2025 "Innocence" rework

## Essential Tracks

If you're new to Taiki Nulight, start here:

1. **"Kush (VIP)"** ft. Dread MC (Night Bass) - UK MC energy over rolling bass house. The perfect introduction.
2. **"My Peoples"** with AC Slater (Night Bass, 2016) - The Night Bass anthem that broke him in the US.
3. **"Into Darkness"** (Dim Mak, 2018) - Dark, driving, peak-time material.
4. **"Stay In Your Lane"** (Polydor, 2024) - His 2024 dubstep/bass hybrid showing he hasn't forgotten his roots.
5. **"Innocence 2025"** with NERO (Chaos, 2025) - The future. Classic UK bass meets modern production.

## The NYC Connection

For New York heads, Taiki's become a regular presence. He played Output back in the day, various warehouse spots, and most recently hit Bowery Ballroom in 2026. His sound works particularly well here—NYC crowds get the UK garage references but want the bass weight that US festivals demand. Taiki delivers both.

## The Verdict

Taiki Nulight matters because he's never been a tourist in his own sound. When bass house blew up, he was already there. When dubstep had its moment, he'd lived through it the first time. The Polydor deal and NERO collaboration suggest he's entering a new phase—bigger stages, broader audiences—but the core stays intact. This is UK bass music, executed properly, without compromise.

**Rating: 8.5/10** - For consistency, evolution, and genuine cross-Atlantic credibility.

In a scene full of producers who chase whatever's trending, Taiki Nulight has spent over a decade building something singular. The blueprint is his own.
