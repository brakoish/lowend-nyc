# PEGASSI TURNS 99 SCOTT INTO A BELGIAN TECHNO PRESSURE COOKER

**99 Scott, Brooklyn / March 13, 2026 / Gray Area**

Thursday night at 99 Scott. The warehouse space in Bushwick that always delivers when Gray Area books it. Belgian producer **Pegassi** headlined with support from **Cole Knight** and **Boy Sanders**, and the room was packed well before midnight.

## THE SET

Pegassi opened with atmospheric pads that built into driving 138 BPM techno, the kind of slow burn that rewards patience. His sound pulls from eurodance, trance, techno, and house without sitting neatly in any lane, and in person that range is even more obvious. One moment you're in a classic trance breakdown, the next he's dropped something that sounds like it belongs on a Trym mix.

The **"No Type" remix** hit around 1:30 AM and the room went from moving to absolutely losing it. The track that put him on SoundCloud's #1 spot still hits different in a warehouse setting. His newer material, including what sounded like cuts from the just-released **"MOAA"** (Mother of All Anthems), carried the same festival-sized energy scaled down to an intimate room.

Cole Knight warmed up the main room with a set that moved between acid, house, and hard groove. Miami-based, she's been on a serious run with releases on Life and Death, Higher Ground, and Factory 93. Her set was locked in and unpredictable, exactly what an opening slot should be.

Boy Sanders started the night with punchy hard house and breaks, setting the tone with deep digs and nostalgic samples over heavy drums.

## STANDOUT MOMENTS

- Cole Knight's acid-laced opener that had the early crowd locked in by 11 PM
- The "No Type" remix drop at peak time, warehouse reverb doing the work
- Pegassi closing with a trance-influenced rework that held the room until 4 AM
- The 99 Scott sound system handling the low end beautifully all night

## THE VERDICT

Pegassi is more than a viral remix. His tracks are staples for Marlon Hoffstadt, Anetha, Trym, Solomun, and Bad Boombox for a reason. The Tomorrowland and Boiler Room sets translated perfectly to a Brooklyn warehouse. With "MOAA" just out and a packed festival run ahead, this was a chance to see him in a 300-cap room before that window closes.

**Rating: 9/10**
